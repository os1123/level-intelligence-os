# Level Intelligence OS - Website and HTML Deck Content

## Creative Direction

Build this as a serious executive microsite or HTML deck for Level Construction leadership.

Visual tone:

- operational, high-trust, and construction-specific;
- clean grids, restrained motion, strong typography, and real workflow artifacts;
- dark charcoal, white, steel gray, muted construction yellow, and restrained green for positive status;
- avoid neon AI styling, floating robot imagery, generic chatbot visuals, and abstract hype graphics.

Core visual metaphor:

> A command layer over Level's real pursuit workflow: signals become opportunities, opportunities become decisions, decisions become operating memory.

Primary UI motifs:

- pipeline table with opportunity score, owner, next action, and age;
- signal cards from permits, planning agendas, RFQs, broker notes, and relationship tips;
- account/contact graph;
- RFQ command center;
- approval queue;
- weekly executive brief;
- feedback loop from outcome back into scoring.

Use this as a Claude Design or OpenSlide source document. Each section can become either a page in a microsite or a slide in a horizontal HTML deck.

## Narrative Arc

1. Level already has the judgment.
2. The judgment is scattered across analog workflows.
3. Construction intelligence tools prove the category, but Level needs more than early alerts.
4. The build uses proven internal construction-platform primitives without exposing prior work.
5. Procore remains the source of truth first; the new layer wraps around it before replacing anything.
6. The market is shifting toward forward-deployed, company-specific AI systems.
7. Level Intelligence OS finds, qualifies, routes, and converts opportunities.
8. The 90-day pilot proves value in a contained operating workflow.
9. The long-term asset is Level's proprietary operating memory.

## Slide/Page 1 - Hero

**Headline:** Level Intelligence OS

**Subheadline:** A private construction intelligence operating system for finding, qualifying, routing, and converting opportunities through Level's own operating judgment.

**Support copy:** Level already has the relationships, market instincts, construction discipline, development experience, and executive judgment. Level Intelligence OS turns that knowledge into a working operating layer.

**Primary CTA:** Start with a 90-day pilot

**Secondary CTA:** Review the operating model

**Visual direction:** Full-width command-center interface. Left side shows incoming signals. Center shows opportunity scoring. Right side shows next actions and executive decisions. Use Level branding cues if available, but keep the system productized and premium.

**Layout notes:** Strong title in first viewport. Under it, three compact proof points: "Early signals," "Level-specific qualification," "Executive decision rhythm."

## Slide/Page 2 - The Problem

**Headline:** Level's advantage is real. It is just scattered.

**Copy:** The highest-value information in construction pursuit work rarely lives in one system. It lives in RFQs, emails, calls, spreadsheets, permit threads, broker notes, project folders, and individual memory.

**Callout:** When judgment is scattered, leadership loses speed, visibility, and repeatability.

**Visual direction:** Split the page into messy inputs flowing toward a missing center: RFQ package, permit comment, relationship note, planning agenda, spreadsheet, email thread, executive call note.

**Key bullets:**

- Active pursuits are hard to compare.
- RFQ risk is buried in documents.
- Relationship context depends on memory.
- Early signals can stall before ownership is clear.
- Decisions are hard to learn from after the fact.

## Slide/Page 3 - Category Context

**Headline:** Construction intelligence is becoming its own operating category.

**Copy:** Tools like Mercator show the value of earlier project intelligence: permits, planning activity, ownership changes, and relationship mapping. That is useful category proof, not the strategic endpoint.

**Level-specific turn:** The goal is not to compete with Mercator or any other vendor. Level needs software built from the ground up around its own relationships, standards, RFQ process, approval rules, markets, capital context, and executive rhythm.

**Quote block:** Level Intelligence OS is not a vendor clone. It is Level-specific internal software that finds, qualifies, routes, and converts opportunities using Level's own operating judgment.

**Visual direction:** Two-column comparison.

| Category Intelligence | Level Intelligence OS |
|---|---|
| Finds early signals | Finds and qualifies signals |
| Maps project activity | Connects activity to Level relationships |
| Supports BD awareness | Supports pursuit, RFQ, approval, and executive workflow |
| External data product | Level-owned operating memory |

## Slide/Page 4 - Proven Infrastructure Patterns

**Headline:** We bring proven platform primitives without exposing prior work.

**Copy:** The build can use construction-platform patterns already worked through in prior platform development: connected data models, workflow queues, approval gates, specialist agent handoffs, document extraction, audit logs, dashboard surfaces, and feedback loops.

**Visual direction:** Grid of reusable operating primitives.

| Pattern | Level Application |
|---|---|
| Domain model | Accounts, contacts, sites, RFQs, bids, permits, approvals, and outcomes |
| Workflow state machines | Opportunity, RFQ, permit, approval, and vendor workflows move through explicit statuses |
| Human controls | Approval queues, audit logs, escalation rules, and role-based authority |
| Feedback loops | Wins, losses, overrides, cycle times, and vendor outcomes improve recommendations |

## Slide/Page 5 - Procore Bridge Strategy

**Headline:** Procore is the bridge, not the battlefield.

**Copy:** Level Intelligence OS should wrap around Procore first, not replace it. Procore remains the execution source of truth while the new layer handles intelligence, RFQs, relationships, approvals, executive briefs, and operating memory.

**Visual direction:** Layer diagram: Procore as project record layer; Level Intelligence OS as intelligence and workflow layer above it.

| Phase | Position |
|---|---|
| Read and reference | Use only the Procore data needed for visibility and decisions |
| Workflow overlay | Add signal intake, RFQ command center, relationship memory, and executive brief |
| Evidence-based rationalization | Measure which workflows belong in Level-owned software |
| Selective replacement | Reduce monolith dependency only where there is clear value and low risk |

## Slide/Page 6 - Market Shift

**Headline:** Software is becoming forward-deployed and company-specific.

**Copy:** The market is moving from fixed SaaS screens toward AI systems deployed inside real workflows. OpenAI's Deployment Company and forward-deployed engineering model are a clear signal that enterprise value increasingly comes from workflow fit, deployment expertise, governance, and measurable outcomes.

**Visual direction:** Three-stage transformation: static SaaS -> deployed internal system -> owned operating memory.

**Key bullets:**

- software adapts to the company, not only the other way around;
- language-heavy workflows can now become structured software;
- forward-deployed experts connect models, tools, data, approvals, and operations;
- owned workflow memory becomes part of enterprise value;
- language-native change makes it easier to add or omit modules as Level evolves.

## Slide/Page 7 - The Proposition

**Headline:** A private operating layer around Level's pursuit engine.

**Copy:** Level Intelligence OS gives Level one place to see the signal, understand the fit, route the work, and track the decision.

**Four-part system:**

1. **Find:** detect project, permit, planning, ownership, franchise, broker, and RFQ signals.
2. **Qualify:** score each opportunity against Level's criteria.
3. **Route:** assign owners, next actions, and approvals.
4. **Convert:** support RFQs, go/no-go memos, pursuit briefs, and weekly executive review.

**Visual direction:** Horizontal operating flow with each step shown as a product screen.

**Layout notes:** Use crisp iconography: radar/signal, scorecard, routing arrows, signed approval/check.

## Slide/Page 8 - Outcomes, Not Just Code

**Headline:** We give you outcomes, not just code.

**Copy:** Code is the mechanism. The product is operating lift: cleaner pipeline visibility, faster RFQ triage, fewer missed follow-ups, weekly executive decision rhythm, and reusable operating memory.

**Visual direction:** Outcome scorecard.

| Outcome | What Changes |
|---|---|
| Pipeline visibility | Active pursuits have owner, stage, score, next action, and age |
| RFQ discipline | Deadlines, requirements, missing information, and go/no-go reasoning are extracted and routed |
| Decision speed | Approvals and exceptions surface before they become bottlenecks |
| Executive rhythm | Leadership gets a weekly brief from live system data |
| Enterprise memory | Signals, decisions, outcomes, and overrides improve the next cycle |

## Slide/Page 9 - Opportunity Intelligence

**Headline:** Earlier signals, structured before they become noise.

**Copy:** The system monitors and ingests opportunity signals, then turns them into structured records Level can review and act on.

**Signal sources:**

- permits and planning agendas;
- title and ownership changes;
- rezonings and variances;
- commercial real estate news;
- hospitality and franchise expansion;
- broker notes and relationship tips;
- inbound RFQs;
- CRM/email activity.

**Output fields:** source, date, account, contact, site, market, jurisdiction, project type, confidence, recommended owner, recommended next action.

**Visual direction:** Signal inbox with filters for market, source, score, age, and owner. Show a highlighted signal becoming a qualified opportunity.

## Slide/Page 10 - Level-Specific Qualification

**Headline:** The score should reflect Level's judgment, not a generic model.

**Copy:** Each opportunity is scored against the criteria Level actually uses to decide whether work is worth pursuing.

**Score dimensions:** geography, asset type, project size, timing, relationship strength, owner/developer quality, entitlement complexity, financing credibility, margin potential, capacity, prior Level experience, competitive risk, strategic value.

**Outputs:** pursue/watch/pass, confidence, reasoning, missing information, risks, next action, owner, comparable history.

**Visual direction:** Opportunity scorecard with weighted dimensions and plain-English rationale. Include "missing information" and "approval needed" states.

## Slide/Page 11 - Relationship Memory

**Headline:** Relationship context becomes searchable operating memory.

**Copy:** The system connects accounts, contacts, internal relationship owners, prior projects, RFQs, touches, and next follow-ups.

**Questions it answers:**

- Who do we know here?
- Who owns the relationship?
- What have we built, bid, or discussed?
- When was the last meaningful touch?
- Which opportunity is tied to this person?
- What should we say next?

**Visual direction:** Account page with contact graph, timeline, active opportunities, and suggested next touch.

**Layout notes:** Make this feel like a construction relationship command file, not a generic CRM card.

## Slide/Page 12 - RFQ Command Center

**Headline:** Turn RFQs into decision-ready work.

**Copy:** RFQs arrive as language-heavy packages. Level Intelligence OS extracts the operating facts and prepares the go/no-go decision.

**Capabilities:**

- scope summary;
- deadline extraction;
- required attachments;
- missing information;
- schedule and site risk;
- comparable past work;
- bid tasks;
- clarification tracking;
- go/no-go memo;
- final outcome capture.

**Visual direction:** RFQ detail page with extracted requirements on the left, risk flags in the center, and approval/go-no-go panel on the right.

## Slide/Page 13 - Human Approval Model

**Headline:** The system prepares. Level decides.

**Copy:** Consequential actions remain under human control. The system drafts, recommends, routes, and logs. Leadership and operators approve the moves that matter.

**Authority table:**

| Level | Examples |
|---|---|
| Autonomous | summarize RFQ, classify signal, dedupe account |
| Suggest | recommend owner, next action, pursue/watch/pass |
| Approval required | outreach, bid submission, vendor award, budget change |
| Manual | legal, financing, contracts, final strategic decisions |

**Visual direction:** Approval queue with recommendation, evidence, confidence, approver, and outcome.

## Slide/Page 14 - Weekly Executive Brief

**Headline:** Leadership sees what changed, what is stuck, and what needs judgment.

**Copy:** The weekly brief replaces status gathering with decision support.

**Brief sections:**

- top new signals;
- active opportunities by stage;
- highest-scoring pursuits;
- RFQs due soon;
- stalled items;
- pending approvals;
- relationship follow-ups;
- permit or entitlement risks;
- key changes since last week;
- decisions needed.

**Visual direction:** Executive brief screen with three columns: "Changed," "Stuck," "Needs decision." Include a concise PDF/email export version.

## Slide/Page 15 - 90-Day Pilot

**Headline:** A contained pilot with visible operating value.

**Copy:** The pilot should prove usefulness in the workflows Level already runs: opportunity review, RFQ triage, relationship follow-up, approval routing, and executive reporting.

| Period | Focus | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1-2 | Foundation | Data model, active pursuits, accounts/contacts, executive view |
| Weeks 3-5 | Intelligence | Signal inbox, summaries, scoring, relationship briefs |
| Weeks 6-8 | Workflow | RFQ command center, go/no-go memo, approval queue |
| Weeks 9-12 | Rhythm | Weekly brief, outcome capture, adoption review, roadmap |

**Visual direction:** Timeline with product screens appearing at each milestone.

## Slide/Page 16 - Success Metrics

**Headline:** Success is measured by operating clarity.

**Metrics:**

- 100 percent of active pursuits visible.
- Each pursuit has owner, stage, source, score, next action, and age.
- At least 10 useful signals captured, reviewed, and routed.
- RFQs tracked with deadline, requirements, and go/no-go status.
- Weekly executive brief generated from live system data.
- Leadership identifies faster decisions, fewer blind spots, or clearer accountability.

**Visual direction:** KPI strip plus before/after workflow. Avoid vanity AI metrics.

## Slide/Page 17 - Phase Two

**Headline:** Expand where the pilot proves value.

**Potential modules:**

- permit and entitlement monitoring;
- CRM, email, calendar, and storage integration;
- Procore read-only bridge around the current execution source of truth;
- vendor and subcontractor performance memory;
- project handoff from pursuit to execution;
- finance and capital workflow;
- hospitality pipeline tracking;
- investor reporting support;
- software rationalization and tool-usage audit.

**Copy:** Phase two should be based on evidence from Level's actual workflows, not a preset software wish list.

**Visual direction:** Modular roadmap with Level-controlled expansion paths.

## Slide/Page 18 - Ownership And Trust

**Headline:** Level owns the operating memory.

**Copy:** The strategic asset is not a model provider. It is Level's structured operating knowledge: relationships, signals, decisions, outcomes, scoring logic, and workflow history.

**Principles:**

- Level owns its data and generated operating memory.
- AI providers remain replaceable infrastructure.
- Export rights should be preserved.
- Role-based access and audit logs are required.
- Sensitive actions require explicit approval.

**Visual direction:** Secure data layer under the product interface. Use lock, audit trail, and export motifs without making it look like a cybersecurity product.

## Slide/Page 19 - Close

**Headline:** Build the system that makes Level sharper every week.

**Copy:** The first version creates visibility and decision speed. The long-term version becomes Level's proprietary construction intelligence layer.

**CTA:** Approve a 90-day pilot.

**Discovery asks:**

1. Active opportunity list and current pursuit stages.
2. Sample RFQs and bid packages.
3. Account/contact export or representative relationship list.
4. Current tools and data sources.
5. Leadership's current weekly reporting format.
6. Criteria Level uses to pursue, watch, or pass.
7. Approval rules for outreach, bids, vendors, budgets, and investor communication.

**Visual direction:** Final command-center screen with the phrase: "Find. Qualify. Route. Convert. Learn."
