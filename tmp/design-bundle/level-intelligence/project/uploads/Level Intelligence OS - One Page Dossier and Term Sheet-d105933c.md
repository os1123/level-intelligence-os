# Level Intelligence OS - One Page Dossier and Term Sheet

## One-Line Proposition

**Level Intelligence OS is a private construction intelligence operating system that helps Level find, qualify, route, and convert opportunities using Level's own operating judgment.**

## Why This Matters

Level's advantage is not just construction execution. It is the judgment inside the company: relationships, market instincts, bid discipline, development experience, hospitality knowledge, capital awareness, vendor memory, and the ability to move quickly when the right opportunity appears.

Today, much of that advantage lives across email, RFQs, spreadsheets, project folders, permit threads, calls, and individual memory.

Level Intelligence OS turns that knowledge into a Level-owned operating layer.

## Category Context

Mercator is a useful reference point for the category: early construction opportunity intelligence.

But the goal is not to compete with Mercator or any other vendor. The goal is to build from the ground up around Level's own needs: its relationships, pursuit standards, RFQ process, approval rules, development instincts, hospitality pipeline, capital context, and executive operating rhythm.

Level Intelligence OS is different in scope and intent because it is Level-specific internal software:

> Mercator finds opportunities. Level Intelligence OS finds, qualifies, routes, and converts opportunities using Level's own relationships, standards, and operating history.

## What Level Gets

| System Area | Client-Facing Outcome |
|---|---|
| Signal inbox | Early project, permit, planning, ownership, franchise, broker, and RFQ signals captured in one place |
| Opportunity graph | Accounts, contacts, sites, jurisdictions, RFQs, bids, permits, and decisions connected |
| Qualification engine | Pursue, watch, or pass recommendations based on Level-specific fit criteria |
| Procore bridge | Wrap around Procore first as the current source of truth, then reduce monolith dependence only where Level-owned modules prove stronger |
| Relationship memory | Searchable account/contact context, relationship owners, last touch, and next follow-up |
| RFQ command center | Scope summaries, deadlines, missing requirements, go/no-go memos, and bid tasks |
| Approval queue | Consequential actions routed to humans before external movement |
| Executive brief | Weekly view of what changed, what is stuck, what needs action, and what leadership must decide |

## What This Is Not

This is not a chatbot demo, generic dashboard, CRM migration, forced Procore replacement, or one-off automation.

This is a private operating system around Level's pursuit, preconstruction, relationship, development, and executive workflows.

## Procore-First Strategy

The recommended first move is to structure the platform around Procore, not replace it. Procore can remain the execution source of truth while Level Intelligence OS adds the intelligence, RFQ, relationship, approval, and executive layer around it.

The long-term opportunity is to reduce reliance on monolithic software module by module. That should happen only after Level's owned system proves a workflow can be handled with better fit, lower friction, and clear ROI.

## 90-Day Pilot

**Objective:** Prove that Level Intelligence OS improves opportunity visibility, qualification discipline, RFQ coordination, executive reporting, and institutional memory.

| Period | Focus | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1-2 | Foundation | Data model, roles, active opportunity/account/contact views, executive dashboard skeleton |
| Weeks 3-5 | Intelligence | Signal inbox, opportunity summaries, qualification scoring, relationship briefs, first weekly digest |
| Weeks 6-8 | Workflow | RFQ command center, deadline extraction, go/no-go memo, approval queue, bid task tracking |
| Weeks 9-12 | Operating rhythm | Weekly executive brief, outcome capture, adoption review, phase-two roadmap |

## Pilot Success Metrics

- 100 percent of active pursuits visible with owner, stage, source, score, next action, and age.
- At least 10 useful signals captured, reviewed, and routed.
- RFQs tracked with deadlines, requirements, and go/no-go status.
- Weekly executive brief generated from live system data.
- Leadership can point to faster decisions, fewer blind spots, or clearer accountability.
- Phase-two roadmap is based on observed value, not assumptions.

## Term Sheet Skeleton

**Parties:** Level X / Level Construction / related Level operating entities and builder/advisor TBD.

**Engagement:** 90-day pilot for a private, Level-specific construction intelligence operating system.

**Pilot deliverables:**

1. Level data model and workflow map.
2. Opportunity, account, and contact database.
3. Procore-first bridge strategy.
4. Signal inbox and triage workflow.
5. Level-specific qualification scoring framework.
6. Relationship intelligence view.
7. RFQ intake and go/no-go memo workflow.
8. Human approval queue.
9. Weekly executive brief.
10. Pilot success report and phase-two roadmap.

**Optional phase two:**

- permit and entitlement monitoring;
- CRM, email, calendar, and storage integration;
- Procore read-only bridge around the current execution source of truth;
- vendor and subcontractor performance memory;
- finance/capital workflow;
- hospitality pipeline tracking;
- investor reporting support;
- software rationalization and tool-usage audit.

**Ownership principles:**

- Level owns its data, operating memory, domain model, and workflow definitions.
- Level retains export rights for pilot data and generated records.
- Code ownership/licensing to be defined in the final agreement.
- AI model providers remain replaceable infrastructure.

**Data and confidentiality:**

- Level data is confidential.
- Role-based access and audit logging required.
- No external reuse of Level data without written permission.
- External outreach, bid submission, vendor award, budget change, investor communication, legal, and financing actions require human approval.

**Commercial structure to define after discovery:**

- fixed-fee 90-day pilot;
- build fee plus monthly operating retainer;
- strategy/design sprint followed by implementation proposal;
- long-term internal software partnership.

## Closing Message

The first version gives Level cleaner visibility and faster decisions. The strategic version gives Level a proprietary operating memory that improves how the company finds, qualifies, wins, and executes work.
